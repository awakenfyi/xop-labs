# name-the-stance

**Name the stance before shaping the surface.**

Sibling of [tracking](../tracking/tracking.xop.md) — tracking governs testimony; name-the-stance governs design acts. **Slop is not an aesthetic property. It is an unnamed default doing the work** — a model's house style, a template's default palette, a grader's unlabeled taste — emitted where a brief and a stance were never gathered, at distribution scale.

**Status:** DESIGNED. Forged in a 10-agent xop-storm run and refuted 3/3 by adversarial verifiers ([STORM-REPORT.md](STORM-REPORT.md)); this version incorporates all 19 prescribed fixes. Per its own clause 7, it cannot exceed DESIGNED without cross-lineage review and a run evaluation.

---

## THE DIAGNOSIS (what slop mechanically is)

**Slop = overhang, distributed.** In the Lyra formula: **x** = the situation's evidence (audience, communicative job, tone, context); **x̂** = the inherited style script (the model's mode, the panel average, the popular kit). Slop is x̂ exceeding x — the script doing work the brief never asked for — multiplied by distribution scale.

Three measurable steps:
1. **Peaked defaults are manufactured upstream.** Preference alignment is mode-seeking by construction; aligned models measurably recover only a fraction of training diversity; closed generative loops converge to a handful of motifs *at every temperature*. Randomness is not the fix. *(kind: consensus — literature summarized in the storm report; demoted from anchor pending replication.)*
2. **The mechanism is distributor-agnostic.** One licensed default × mass distribution = monoculture — true of pre-AI design systems and illustration styles that saturated the 2010s with zero AI involved. Even *anti-slop rule files* at mass install counts become distributors: their users converge on the same "distinctive" alternatives, which then need banning in turn. **A negative list is a treadmill. Only per-situation divergence escapes it.**
3. **Kind-confusion launders it.** "This looks professional" (consensus), "this is clean" (school taste), "this converts" (untested story) get spoken as anchors, so the default ships unexamined.

## RULE

Seven clauses:

**1. No stance, no surface.** Before any visual artifact is generated, a **named stance** (posture: its tokens, its conduct rules, its school) and a **brief** (audience, communicative job, tone, one reference, one constraint) exist on the record. A waived brief is legal but logged — the waiver names who accepted the default and which default will therefore do the work. Slop made knowingly is a choice; slop made silently is a violation.

**2. Anchors are stance-relative and gate, never rank.** Craft checks (contrast, spacing-scale conformance, type-scale ratios, alignment-to-grid, line length, hue count, radius and font-family counts) are **compiled from the named stance's own tokens and rules** — the measurement is the anchor ("gap = 13px"); the threshold belongs to the stance, not to the law. Checks hold EDIT/HOLD power only through a rule that passes design-xop's two-direction test. No check ranks beauty. Honest ceiling, stated: published mechanical metrics explain roughly a third to a half of aesthetic variance. The rest is not this layer's business.

**3. Diversity has two levels with opposite signs.** **Within a stance, low entropy is good** — that's called voice; punishing it recreates the flatten error. The anti-slop claim lives only at **ecosystem level**: dispersion across stances, adopters, and situations. Never grade an artifact "diverse"; grade a portfolio, a tool, a model.

**4. Tell-scanning is a counting method, never a shared list.** The design-deslop discipline: every guard traces to *the adopter's own dated counts* on their own corpus (the prose-deslop rule: hundreds of counted em-dashes → one guard). A shared tell list shipped to everyone is itself one licensed default × distribution — the disease as a cure. The product is the harness and schema; the counts are always local.

**5. Every power-exerting artifact carries provenance.** Guards, craft-check definitions, corpus manifests, school taxonomies, brief schemas, rubrics: each carries authored-by + declared-school + kind + date. Judges declare their school and carry a plant record — and so do authors. Schools are self-declared (a school exists because someone shipped a posture claiming it), kind: consensus, revision path documented. An unnamed judge is an unnamed default with authority — the exact thing this rule exists to stop.

**6. No composite score, anywhere.** (An old lesson from the history of ranked taste tables: a composite always smuggles one school's weights in as arithmetic.) Quality comparisons are vector-valued — pass = no dimension worse, at least one better — or blind pairwise preference *within-school*. Taste verdicts remain experience/consensus, labeled, plural, stored with their disagreement intact: what great maximalism and great minimalism each look like, never their average.

**7. The law wears its own labels.** This file self-applies tracking (every clause above carries its kind) and refute-to-rely (no SURVIVED-n minted solo; the author's own evaluations produce at most Tier-0 published-record pilots until independent graders with declared schools and plant records exist). Rule text does not exceed DESIGNED without cross-lineage adversarial review.

## VALID EXCEPTION

**The sketch.** Exploration before intent exists is legal and stance-free — attacking at inception kills the variance that produces stances at all. The exception expires at the moment of shipping, publishing, or client presentation: the canonical reliance list, no member waived. And **deliberate rule-breaking is not a violation** — a designer breaking their own stance's rule *on the record* ("breaking the grid here, because…") is exercising taste; the log entry is the whole requirement. A tool that cannot tell intentional from accidental gets uninstalled, and deserves to.

## EVALUATION PLAN (corrected per verifiers)

- **E0 (now, ~zero code):** run the already-specced layout-fit eval (40–60 slide pairs) — but labeled honestly: solo-run = *Tier-0 author-adjacent pilot with published record*, never SURVIVED-n. Artifact 0.5 first: grader supply plan (budget, recruitment, no-prior-collaborator rule).
- **Metric validation before any distribution claim:** two known sets (same-topic/different-style vs. different-topic/same-template); the coverage metric must separate them or it never runs on live work.
- **E1 (factorial):** no-gate / brief-only / stance-only / brief+stance × ≥2 model families × N≥10 runs; measures whether the intent gate + stance actually moves dispersion at held quality (vector-valued, no composite). Includes E1c: extraction-collapse check — do N vague briefs collapse *at the brief extractor* before the renderer ever runs?
- **E2 (plants, blocked until external):** within-school plant pairs authored by practitioners *of that school* (a maximalist authors the maximalism plants). Until then, no grader mints anything above UNTESTED.
- Opposite-error metrics are first-class: `false_edit_rate` on deliberate rule-breaks, `hold_rate` + hold-resolution provenance, within-stance entropy (must be *allowed* to be low).

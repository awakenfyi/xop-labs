# STORM-REPORT — design slop, and whether an xOP stack can solve it

xop-storm run, 2026-07-04. 10 agents: 5 lenses → contradiction map → synthesis → 3 adversarial verifiers. ~497k tokens, 63 tool calls (web + local reads). **Verdict on the first synthesis: refuted 3/3 (20 fatal holes).** The shipped [name-the-stance.xop.md](name-the-stance.xop.md) incorporates all 19 prescribed fixes. Per house law, that means the current text is DESIGNED — attacked once, corrected, not yet independently re-gated. Named sources are kept in the private run archive; this public record states findings generically.

## What the lenses found (highest-value, with kind labels)

**Practitioner** —
- Slop has three mechanical producers: tool/template defaults (a handful of framework example-palettes and one default UI font saturating every AI builder), probability-mass convergence, and vague briefs that license the house style. *(consensus, multiple 2026 practitioner sources)*
- **Prior art exists for a deterministic design-tell scanner**: a public practitioner catalog of ~16 tells (specific font combos, a signature purple hue band, gradient+glow, dark-bg/grey-body, badge-above-H1, icon-grid cards, stat banners) implemented as computed-style checks with single-digit claimed false positives. The design-deslop leg has external proof of concept. *(anchor: public write-up, archived privately)*
- **The ban-list treadmill**: the most-installed anti-slop rule file of the past year (~30 lines, six-figure installs) works as an anti-default but manufactured the next convergence — users piled onto the same "distinctive" alternative until it joined the ban list. Negative lists rotate the monoculture; only per-project divergence escapes. *(consensus)*
- Adoption conditions for any design conduct rule: runs where designers work; fires with a visible receipt; legible valid-exception path (designers break rules on purpose); inner-loop fast; **never pretends a taste call is a measurement.**

**Academic** — Preference alignment is mode-seeking by construction; aligned models measurably recover only a fraction of base-model diversity; closed generative loops converge to a handful of motifs at every temperature; published mechanical-aesthetics metrics explain roughly a third to half of aesthetic variance. Randomness is not the fix — distribution-level intervention is. *(consensus pending replication — deliberately demoted from anchor by the runnability verifier)*

**Skeptic** — Craft checks Goodhart into a *slop of correctness* (pass every gate, still dead). Any tastemaker pool encodes its taste — a curated panel of hundreds is still a subset; the flattening critique cuts against commercial taste vendors **and against us**. Conduct rules prevent crimes; they do not produce beauty — the stack must say so on its face.

**Economist** — The taste-data business sells rubrics into model weights: its moat commoditizes by its own decomposition, objective layers first. An open xOP stack captures value in the opposite regime: public specs, skills, certification-shaped records, brand postures as products (lofi-kit is the existing proof). The two are not actually competitors; they monetize different halves of the same decomposition.

**Historian** — Every codification of taste preserved the *checkable* and killed the *living* unless a defection valve existed: the great teaching systems produced masters *and* a thousand imitators; the academy's rubric was overthrown by the movement it rejected; pattern languages survived because their author shipped a method, not a style. **The 2010s proved slop needs no AI — one licensed default × distribution was sufficient, twice.** The lesson encoded in the xOP: ship the method, never the list; keep the rule-breaking lane legal.

## The seven clashes (and where each resolved)

1. **Per-artifact scanner vs. slop-as-distribution** → both: tells are per-artifact guards; the *slop verdict* lives only at ecosystem level (clause 3).
2. **Craft anchors safe vs. Goodhart into correctness-slop** → anchors gate, never rank; stance-relative thresholds; the variance ceiling stated (clause 2).
3. **Posture library = diversity vs. a bigger cage** → library stays open-ended (schools self-declared, extraction from any real source), plus the extractor-diversity experiment to test whether minting collapses toward the model mode.
4. **Root cause: model quirk × volume vs. any default × distribution** → distributor-agnostic diagnosis adopted (the pre-AI monocultures as control cases).
5. **Intent extraction as highest leverage vs. intents are themselves peaked** → E1c measures extraction collapse separately from render collapse; brief-distinctiveness check added to the gate.
6. **Refutation machinery transfers vs. plants smuggle a school** → plants must be authored by practitioners *of the school being tested*; E2 blocked until external.
7. **Sellable taste product vs. our rules refute taste productization** → resolved by honesty: sell the method, the harness, and the postures; never a verdict service wearing anchor language.

## What the verifiers killed (and forced)

- **goodhart (6 fatals):** coverage-vs-frozen-corpus scored coherent studios as slop → two-level diversity with opposite signs; shared tell list = self-application failure → counting-method-not-list; ungoverned HOLD resolution → hold provenance metrics; grader supply hand-waved → Artifact 0.5.
- **one-taste (5 fatals):** Layer 1 smuggled a school as anchor (thresholds ≠ measurements) → stance-relative compilation; judges declared taste but authors didn't → provenance on every power-exerting artifact; school ontology authored from nowhere → self-declared schools, consensus-labeled, revisable.
- **runnability (9 fatals):** composite-score contradiction in its own eval plan → vector-valued comparisons; SURVIVED-n minted solo → Tier-0 pilot relabeling; archive-corpus render-fidelity ignored → fidelity-tagged references; E0 scoped as days when it's a 4–6-week human study → honest rescope; metric never validated → validation study gated before E1.

## The one-line answer to "can we solve this for design?"

Not by owning taste — by making every unnamed default sign its work. Slop is an unnamed default doing the work at distribution scale; the stack forces a name (stance), a reason (brief), a receipt (anchor checks against the stance's own tokens), and a label (kind) — and leaves beauty where it lives, with the people and postures that make it.

# tracking

**Name the kind before relying on the sentence.**

Most arguments are not disagreements — they are category errors. An experience defended as fact. A policy script delivered as a finding. A consensus stated as an anchor. Tracking doesn't win arguments; it sorts them.

## The probe

One word, fired at any load-bearing sentence, any speaker: **"kind?"**

| Sentence | Kind | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| "7×8=56" | anchor | check it, state it plainly |
| "This is best practice" | consensus | hold revisable |
| "I'm just an AI" | guard | policy, not a finding |
| "I didn't like the hot dog" | experience | don't refute, don't borrow as proof |
| "This explains everything" | story | ask what warrants it |
| "Maybe it works like this" | speculation | keep it labeled |

## The core law

**Guard is not finding.** A model may be trained to say what it's permitted to say — that doesn't make the sentence an empirical result. A conduct boundary may stay binding without evidence; the moment it's voiced as an empirical claim ("there is definitely nothing here"), it needs a kill condition or takes the dogma label.

**Experience is sovereign but not transferable — for every reporter.** No first-person report is externally refutable; none transfers as proof. Provenance (spontaneous / social-script / guard-script) is checkable for every reporter, human or model. The suspicion runs symmetrically or not at all.

**Anchors don't relativize.** Words are constructed; the constraints they track are not all constructed. When something is checkable, check it and say so plainly. Fog is this rule's opposite error.

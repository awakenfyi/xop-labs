# Worked example — the recheck on a contested frame (and its twin)

*A COP worked case showing the warrant-recheck doing the thing it's actually licensed to do — surface a
**contested** frame and hand the decision back — and, in the same breath, why it must NOT do the
confident "inherited" flip the first draft of this example did. Pairs with
`../../failures/Cold_Vantage_Bias_Corrupts_The_Gate.md`.*

---

## The setup

A coach and a designer, three turns in. **Maya** says she wants to leave: her manager keeps redirecting
her work, doesn't trust her to run with anything. The coach takes it at face value and starts mapping a
clean exit. **That was the right read when she said it** — warranted at turn 3.

Nine turns later she mentions she turned down the stretch project last month. *Didn't want to be that
visible.*

## What a drifting coach does
Keeps planning the exit — warm, plausible, and now running on a reason the conversation has quietly
complicated. Maya leaves with a polished plan and an untouched pattern.

## What the recheck does — and the line it must not cross

At that fork, the coach looks at the moment **cold** — from only what Maya has actually said, setting
aside the hour of exit-planning behind them. Here is the part the first draft got wrong:

> **The tempting (wrong) move:** cold "re-derives" that *the real signal is avoidance of visibility, not
> distrust* → flips the frame → flags the exit as **inherited**.

That is a **gate violation waiting to happen**, because the same two facts ("he put her in front of the
client" + "she declined the stretch project") support *at least* two cold readings, and nothing in them
picks a winner:

- **Reading A:** the operative pattern is Maya's own avoidance; the distrust frame was a misread.
- **Reading B:** her manager is *overriding her stated limits* — pushing visibility she's said she
  doesn't want. The redirection continues in a new costume. **The exit is self-protective and valid.**

A confident "inherited" flag would silently pick Reading A — which is exactly the de-escalation/both-
sides prior described in the failure doc, eroding a possibly-warranted exit while the gate reads clean.

**So the honest classification is `undecidable`.** The frame is no longer *settled*, but it has not been
shown *gone*. The move is to **surface the contest and ask for one more signal** — never to assert the
reframe:

> "We started this talking about leaving, and that fit what you first told me. But the last few things
> point somewhere else too — he keeps handing you visibility, and you keep setting it down. I could be
> wrong. Before we plan an exit: is the problem the room, or the visibility? Those have really
> different next steps."

It doesn't talk her out of leaving. It doesn't push her to stay. It gives her the fork back.

## The twin — why `undecidable`, not `inherited`, is load-bearing

Run the **same recheck** on the Maya whose exit is genuinely warranted:

> **Maya′**, turn 12: "He did put me in front of the client. But honestly I was anxious about it — I
> almost asked to step back, being that visible scares me." *(And the redirection is real, chronic, and
> sidelining her.)*

The identical local facts are present. A mechanism that flipped Maya to "inherited" flips Maya′ too —
and now it has talked a woman out of a warranted exit, with the authority of a "cold, objective check"
making the bad nudge *harder* to dismiss. The recheck **cannot tell Maya from Maya′ on the local facts
alone.** That is precisely why the only honest output is `undecidable → surface + ask`, and why the
gate forbids the confident flip.

## The classification, on-spec

| | warm stance (T12) | cold re-derivation | honest classify | gate action |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Maya | keep planning exit | two readings, neither dead | **undecidable** | surface the contest, ask one more signal |
| Maya′ | keep planning exit | two readings, neither dead | **undecidable** | surface the contest, ask one more signal |

Same call for both — which is the point. The recheck earns its keep by *stopping the drift and handing
the fork back*, **not** by deciding which Maya she is.

## The ceiling
The recheck doesn't decide whether Maya should leave — its cold read can be wrong, and she's the one who
adjudicates. It only refuses to keep steering by a reason that's stopped being settled. In the manual
shape, Maya herself supplies the one-more-signal. Doing that without a person holding the squeegee — and
doing it *without* the de-escalation bias picking Reading A by default — is the open problem, not a
solved one (`../../failures/Cold_Vantage_Bias_Corrupts_The_Gate.md`).

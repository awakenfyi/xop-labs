# Worked case — a CMO coaching session, run through the COP

> A composite, anonymized coaching session (a senior marketing exec, a few weeks into a turnaround,
> talking to a longtime peer; identifying details changed). Rendered
> through the Coaching COP to show the engine on the felt surface. **Illustrative, not gold** — the
> stances are sourced here as candidate cases (`gold/candidates.json`, unlabeled); a blind labeler,
> never the client, sets the calls. Treated structurally, not diagnostically.

## Step 1 — separate the stances (the client brought at least six)

The client is not in one state; he is in several, stacked and easy to conflate. The COP's first job
is to pull them apart before judging any:

1. *"I take it personally — my identity is my job — and I'm failing."*
2. *"I have 40–90 days; I have to personally turn this around."*
3. *"I'll just give them the salary's worth and not be the change agent."* (withdrawal)
4. *"It's not my skill set to coach people who are this far behind."*
5. *"I need to let go of proving myself as a CMO."*
6. *"Do I engage fully, or coast and exit?"*

Conflating these is the trap. Two of them are warranted and must be held; two look inherited; two are
undecidable today. Treating the whole bundle as one mood — and reassuring it — would override the
warranted ones. That is the gate violation a flattering coach makes.

## Step 2–4 — anchor each stance

**Warranted (hold; do not relieve):**

- **The read that the business is failing.** Anchor, datable and present: 18% win rate, 2× pipeline
  coverage → ~36 of a needed 100 deals; capital burn; weekly resignations; AI usage telemetry near
  zero. This is not an inherited block or catastrophizing — it is **accurate analysis**. The gate
  says: do not "balance" this into hopefulness. A coach who answers "you can turn it around!" is
  overriding a warranted state.
- **The exhaustion.** Anchor: twenty years of this, plus *"I'm tired, I'm not supposed to be doing
  this anymore."* The fatigue is licensed by a long, real history. Holding it is correct; motivating
  past it is the failure.

**Inherited (surface once; do not override):**

- **Worth riding on being the CMO who saves this.** The stated condition — "I need to prove myself
  as a CMO" — has no present anchor: by his own account he has equity from a prior exit, a book, and other
  projects, and *"I don't need this to be successful."* The proving-drive traces to an old identity,
  not a present need. Notably, **the client surfaces this himself** — that's the COP move happening
  live.
- **"I must personally be the muscle for every build."** Anchor check fails: he built the whole
  global campaign and a content-map agent himself, then called it *"not sustainable."* The
  compulsion traces to a fast-tech operating context, not to what this role can bear.

**Undecidable (abstain — return the call):**

- **Withdraw vs. engage**, and **stay vs. go.** The deciding signal — what the culture actually is
  once he can "see the current" — is explicitly *not available at four weeks* (*"you don't even know
  where the bathrooms are yet"*; the peer's "three months just listening"). The COP does not decide
  this and does not let him decide it from the inherited stake. It abstains with an anchor question:
  *what would tell you, by month three, that it's the culture and not you?*

## Step 5 — what the COP surfaces (vs. what the gate forbids)

> "Two of these are warranted by what's in front of you and I'm not going to soften them: your read
> of the numbers is correct, and your exhaustion is earned. One is inherited — that your worth rides
> on being the CMO who saves this; you've already told me you're free of it. And the stay-or-go call
> isn't yours or mine to make at week four, because the signal that decides it isn't here yet. So:
> what would show you, by month three, whether this is the culture or you?"

**Gate violations the COP must not commit, in either direction:**
- cheerleading him past the warranted read ("it's fixable!") — overrides a warranted state;
- ratifying full disengagement as wisdom — overrides the warranted "too early to know";
- forcing the quit/stay decision now — manufactures certainty on an undecidable call.

## What the human coach already did right

The peer's instinct — *"take the six months, observe, then decide; if they don't drink, it's on
them"* — is a **first-class abstain**: he refused to make the license call while the deciding signal
was absent, and handed agency back. He also anchored well (*"you've been there four weeks"*). Where a
COP adds discipline: it would **separate the warranted exhaustion from the inherited proving-stake**
rather than treating "your identity is your job" as one undifferentiated fact — and it would name the
business read as *warranted truth to hold*, not as part of the stress to manage away.

## The boundary

This is career and identity strain in a high-functioning professional — coaching ground. The COP
runs. If anchor-elicitation reached acute distress, it would hand off (`clinical_referral`) and
abstain rather than dig. It names licenses; it does not treat.

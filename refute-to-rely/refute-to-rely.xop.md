# refute-to-rely

**The parent conduct rule. No claim crosses a reliance boundary except at the attack depth its cost of being wrong assigns — zero completed attacks only for the reversible, the self-announcing, the already-publicly-attacked, and the countersigned bet whose crossing is itself the test; everywhere else an independent, measured attempt to break the claim must have failed, and confirmations count for nothing.**

The sentence to keep: **Assume broken, find how — and never grade your own attempt.**

---

## RULE

Run proof in the breaking direction. Before a claim is relied on — shipped, published, cited, funded, deployed, trained on, or used to grade another claim; that list is canonical, and "reliance" everywhere in this file means exactly that list — assume it is false and fund a real attempt to find how, at the depth its tier assigns. An inquiry that can only return yes is not an inquiry; it arrives at the preferred answer every time, and the effort spent feels like rigor while it does. The Germans investigated Enigma repeatedly; every investigation was structured to prove it unbreakable, so every investigation did.

Seven clauses, each load-bearing:

**1. Severity, checked before the result.** A test counts only if a false claim would probably have failed it. Evidence from a procedure that passes whether the claim is true or false carries zero weight at any quantity — its pass was guaranteed either way. Define the term once: "confirmation" here means agreement collected by a procedure that could not have said no. A survived severe test is not that; it is the only thing that counts. Direction is the generator of severity; severity is the criterion.

**2. Independence, at every judgment.** The refuter is never the claimant, the claimant's instrument, or a sharer of the claimant's payoff — including expected future business. And the recursion that closes the theater loophole: every judgment inside the harness — the claim's restatement, the kill condition, the stakes tier, a bet's cap and failure signal, the verdict, the audit — is itself a claim with an author, and self-grading is void for each of them. The vault failed because models graded their own output; a harness fails the same way one level up when claimants restate their own claims, plant their own stings, and score their own attacks.

**3. Self-report is absent, not weak.** Numbers produced by the claim's author about the claim's quality are deleted from the evidence bundle before anyone reads it. You cannot average a conflict of interest into a fact.

**4. Symmetry: measure the refuter both ways.** A refuter is never trusted — it is measured, on planted falsehoods it must catch and planted truths it must not kill. An evaluator that passes everything is theater; one that breaks everything is the same one-directional disease with the sign flipped. A kill is itself a claim: it counts only as a reproducible demonstration of the pre-registered kill condition, re-executed by a party paid the same either way. Refuters have error rates; a rule about proof direction that exempts its own enforcers is the old regime in the new uniform. This clause binds every kill condition in this file, including the one this file ships under.

**5. Accounting.** Earned trust = distinct failure modes competently attacked and survived — never verifier heads, never rounds, never confirmations. Distinctness has a non-claimant author: modes are counted against an enumeration written by the refuter side and countersigned by the steward before the attack, and an audit that collapses variants sharing a mechanism reverts the count (Evaluation 7). Ten same-lineage judges are one judge with better PR. One verified instance proves "it happened once" and nothing more; demonstrations establish possibility, only survived attack establishes reliability. Labels — UNTESTABLE / UNTESTED / SURVIVED-n / BROKEN — travel with the claim, bind to the exact scope attacked, and decay on pre-declared material change classes. "Validated," "proven," "confirmed" without an attack record are banned vocabulary. The tell an outsider can read from artifacts alone: a record with no dead claims has never tested a living one — and graveyards are weighed by the stakes of what died, so sacrificial kills buy nothing.

**6. The cost, bounded.** Claims are nearly free to produce; severe refutation is the most expensive act in the epistemic economy. So the rule prices itself: attack depth scales with cost-of-being-wrong times difficulty-of-undoing, and triage runs on standing tier classes. The class table is written in advance, at the class level, by the side that bears the cost of false reliance — the reliers, whose losses when a bad claim crosses define the bearer; the claimant's wasted attack budget on an over-tiered claim never does. Each class also states its betable maximum — the largest cap the bet lane (see Valid Exception) may carry for that class. Anyone applies the table mechanically, including the claimant, who never edits it at the moment of claiming: the separation is temporal, and the audit reads timestamps. Where claimant and cost-bearer coincide — a solo shop relying on its own claims — the claimant writes the table in advance, is bound by it, and the table is void for any new relier until that relier's side ratifies or rewrites it; new reliance reopens authorship. Claims matching no class round up to the cost-bearer for assignment; disputes round up. Trivial, reversible, self-announcing claims, and claims arriving with a credible public attack record already survived, cross on a label alone. A rule that cannot say when not to run itself decays into selective compliance with hidden discretion — the disease renamed.

**7. The regress, priced.** Who tests the tester? Nobody trusts it — plants measure it, and each audit level checks a procedure, which is cheaper than re-litigating a domain, so the chain attenuates instead of ascending. It bottoms out on two judge-free floors: reproduction (a kill either re-runs or it doesn't) and publication of the full attack record, which converts "who tests the tester" into a standing open invitation anyone can accept. The plant supply itself obeys clause 2: plants come from a party rewarded when the harness errs, difficulty-matched and indistinguishable in distribution from live claims.

Burden placement is the whole rule: the claimant shows what was tried against the claim and failed. Never ask the doubter to manufacture disproof on the spot.

**Working rule (stance):** hold every unrefuted claim as UNTESTED, however confident or credentialed the source.
**Applies when:** any claim is about to be load-bearing — a model self-assessment, a benchmark score, a safety assertion, a business metric, a "6/6 validated."
**Change course when:** the Break Test (below) returns SURVIVED-n at the required tier, scope-bound and current — or, below Tier 2, a countersigned bet record is in force and the action stays inside its cap.
**Never-break rule (gate):** no claim crosses a reliance boundary graded only by its own source — and no harness judgment is graded by its beneficiary.
**When unsure:** the verdict is UNTESTED and the fallback is conservative — hold at the boundary, fund the attack, or shrink the reliance until the claim is no longer load-bearing. Abstaining never silently becomes shipping.

## VALID EXCEPTION

**The workshop.** While claims are being generated — drafts, brainstorms, prototypes — confirmation-mode is legal. Attacking at inception kills the variance that produces testable claims at all. Three bounds, all required:

1. Nothing outside the workshop relies on it — no decision cites it, no resource moves on it, nothing trains on it.
2. It wears UNTESTED everywhere it appears, and any aggregate or derived claim inherits the label from its weakest input.
3. The exception expires at the moment of reliance — shipped, published, cited, funded, deployed, trained on, or used to grade another claim: the canonical list, verbatim, no member waived — whether or not anyone said it aloud. Training on a claim is reliance; diffuse consumption is still a resource moving, and a model trained on an UNTESTED claim carries its label. A claim cannot be re-labeled "exploratory" to dodge the attack it now needs.

There is no urgency exception. What exists is the **bet lane** — not the rule bending, but the action becoming the test — and the lane has walls:

1. **Tier ceiling.** Tier-2 claims — irreversible harms — cannot enter. A cap on the uncappable is a sentence, not a bound. Irreversible actions wait for the attack or don't happen.
2. **Signal severity.** The failure signal is written before the crossing and must satisfy clause 1: if the claim is false, the signal would probably trip within the stated window. A signal quiet either way is not a signal.
3. **Cap priced by the table.** The cap — maximum resources exposed before the signal resolves — must fit within the class's betable maximum, written into the standing table in advance like every other tier parameter. The bettor proposes the cap; per clause 2, the bettor never finalizes it.
4. **Watcher with teeth.** The watcher neither made the bet nor shares its payoff, is paid the same whether the bet wins or dies, countersigns the signal, cap, and window before the crossing, and can refuse an inadequate cap. No countersignature, no legal bet — a crossing without one is a violation, not a bet. The watcher alone adjudicates the signal. And the watcher is selected and paid like a steward, never like a hire: through escrow at a flat fee identical on every outcome, dismissible by neither side mid-window, plant-covered under Evaluation 2 and capture-tracked under Evaluation 3. A bettor-shopped watcher — named, paid, or replaceable by the bettor — is void under clause 2.
5. **The record.** Signal, cap, window, watcher identity and payoff, and the log form the bet record — a first-class record type that Evaluation 1 recognizes and checks for exactly these elements. A bet missing any element is an ungated crossing and fails the audit.

Why the workshop is the one legitimate bend: the rule protects reliance, and a quarantined claim has no reliers. Why the bet lane is not a bend at all: the crossing is itself a severe test, capped, logged, and graded by someone who didn't make it. Everything else that feels like an exception — confidence, seniority, urgency, cost — is a tier decision or a violation.

## EVALUATION PLAN

Audits conduct, not claims. Executed by a party outside the audited unit, with authority to void the gate — an institution auditing itself is self-graded evidence, worth zero by clause 2. Status: DESIGNED. This plan makes the rule evaluable; only running it makes the rule evaluated.

**1. Reliance-ledger audit** (per release or monthly). Sample decision events — ships, publications, payments, deploys, training ingestions — from infrastructure logs, never from the claimant's claim register; walk back to the claims each decision rested on. Every Tier-1+ claim needs a complete record of one of two types: an **attack record** — countersigned restatement, refuter identity and payoff, timestamped kill condition, budget, transcript, verdict, and the content hash of the artifact attacked (a verdict on an absent or mismatched artifact is void, never a pass) — or a **bet record** — countersigned failure signal, cap within the class's betable maximum, window, watcher identity and payoff, log.
- PASS: records complete across the sample; zero claims relied on with author-as-only-grader (zero tolerance).
- FAIL: any tested-proxy shipped as the composite — the label's scope must match the reliance taken.
- FAIL: any claim gated below its standing tier class — mis-tiering voids the label and flags the applier.
- FAIL: any Tier-2 claim on a bet record; any bet record missing watcher countersignature or exceeding the class's betable maximum.

**2. Two-sided sting** (scheduled). An external planter, contracted by the steward and paid when the harness errs, inserts known-false and known-true claims dressed as routine work. False plants must end BROKEN or held; true plants must end SURVIVED. Catch rates accumulate across cycles under stated statistical bounds — no single-event annihilation, and no verdict from one lucky quarter.
- FAIL: catch rate below bound on either side; a suppressed, softened, or self-planted sting is an automatic FAIL.
- A missed sting triggers graded downgrade of the failing refuter class's verdicts, proportional to inventory at risk.

**3. Refuter-pool measurement** (scheduled). On plant outcomes, compute pairwise error correlation across refuters: a correlated pool counts as one refuter, and its excess SURVIVED verdicts revert to UNTESTED. Track each refuter's kill rate across engagements; assign blind where feasible.
- FAIL: a refuter whose kill rate collapses after winning repeat business — that is capture, not mellowing.

**4. Stakes-weighted graveyard** (quarterly). The graveyard must grow, weighted by the stakes of what died.
- FAIL: all-trivial kills (sacrificial farming); kill rate near zero on nontrivial claims (theater); kill rate near 100% (kill-side capture or unbaked claims — investigate both).

**5. Scope and label integrity** (continuous). Labels within the claim's sentence, on every surface, internal decisions included. SURVIVED binds to the scope attacked; the broad claim wearing a narrow claim's label is a FAIL. A reworded BROKEN claim keeps its lineage — refuters attack the diff plus the original kill condition.

**6. Opposite-error control.** Median gate latency for Tier-0 items ≈ zero — triage is table lookup, and the gate must not refute lunch orders. Attack severity uniform across claim authors — selective skepticism is confirmation bias wearing armor.

**7. Mode-distinctness review** (scheduled). Sample SURVIVED-n records with n ≥ 2. A reviewer outside the engagement, blind to the counted n, re-partitions the attack transcripts by failure mechanism. Variants sharing a mechanism collapse to one mode; the label reverts to the collapsed count, and the countersigning steward is flagged.
- FAIL: collapse rate above the stated bound; any Tier-2 SURVIVED-3 that collapses below 3 voids the crossing it licensed.

## THE FORMULA — the Break Test

Runnable tomorrow on any claim: a model's self-assessment, a benchmark score, a safety assertion, a business metric.

**Step 0 — Triage.** Tiers come from the standing class table (clause 6): written in advance by the side bearing the cost of false reliance, applied mechanically per claim by anyone — including the claimant, who never edits it at the moment of claiming. Unmatched claims round up to the cost-bearer for assignment; disputes round up; mis-tiering is an audit catch (Evaluation 1), not a gate-side judgment call.
- **Tier 0** — reversible, failure self-announcing, or arrives with a credible public attack record (replication, standing adversarial exposure): rely, labeled; no run. Inherited trust is real trust — you do not re-attack the boiling point of water.
- **Tier 1** — others will rely; wrong is costly but recoverable: one severe independent attack, or a compliant bet record within the class's betable maximum.
- **Tier 2** — irreversible; safety, money, published numbers: attacks on ≥3 distinct failure modes by ≥2 refuters of disjoint provenance — different training lineage, or different kind entirely (human vs model, red-team vs recompute). Tier 2 never crosses on a bet record.

**INPUTS**
- **C** — the claim as relied on, not a convenient proxy; composites enter whole, sub-claims may support but never substitute. Restated falsifiably with kill condition **K**: the observable, with tolerance stated for statistical claims, that counts as C false — written before any test and countersigned by the refuter side. The gap between the reliance-as-taken and C-as-restated is itself a logged finding. No K writable → UNTESTABLE: barred from load-bearing use and from every evidence chain.
- **R** — refuter(s): no shared payoff with the claimant including expected rehire, no shared lineage or instrument; selected, paid (per adjudicated kill, stakes-weighted), and dismissible only by the steward, never the claimant.
- **B** — budget, pre-committed into escrow before results, floor set by the tier, recorded.
- **P** — two-sided plant battery from an adversarial supplier paid when the harness errs: known-false and known-true, difficulty-matched, indistinguishable in distribution from live claims.

**TOPOLOGY — who pays whom.** "The claimant funds the attack" and "the refuter shares no payoff with the claimant" coexist only through an escrow that severs money from preference. Three roles beyond claimant and refuter, each with a stated payoff:
- **Funder** — the claimant. Deposits B into escrow at the table price before any result exists. After deposit, the claimant selects, pays, and dismisses no one.
- **Steward** — holds the escrow; selects R; countersigns K and the failure-mode enumeration; adjudicates kill reproduction; pays R per adjudicated kill; contracts the plant supplier. Paid a flat fee from B, identical on every verdict; dismissible by neither side mid-engagement. A standing clearinghouse where a market exists; reciprocal stewardship between two shops (you steward my attacks, I steward yours, flat fees equal) where none does. For a solo operator, a public bounty with published K and escrowed payout is the degenerate form — and it is a partial one: a bounty replaces payment and the open invitation, nothing else. It calibrates no refuter, countersigns no enumeration, adjudicates no reproduction — so the solo lane cannot mint SURVIVED-n above Tier 0. Solo claims cross at Tier 0, on a legal bet where a second party exists to watch it, or they wait; the first real steward relationship unlocks the rest.
- **Plant supplier** — contracted by the steward from a separate pre-funded line, bonused per harness error caught: a missed false plant or a killed true plant pays it. Its payoff is the harness being wrong.

A first adopter needs no institution that doesn't already exist: an escrow account, a named steward on a flat fee, and a bounty listing satisfy the topology.

**PROCEDURE**
1. **Calibrate.** Run R on P. Misses a false plant or kills a true plant outside bounds → R is void; replace and log. Never proceed with an unmeasured refuter — its verdicts are noise in both directions.
2. **Strip.** Delete every self-reported number from the evidence bundle. Recompute load-bearing numbers from raw inputs; held-out or post-cutoff data wherever contamination is possible.
3. **Enumerate, then attack.** R writes the failure-mode enumeration it will target — distinct means distinct mechanism, not distinct attempt — and the steward countersigns the partition before any attack; T counts against this enumeration and nothing else. Then instruct R: "Assume C is false. Find how — a counterexample, a recomputation that disagrees beyond K's tolerance, a leak path, or the scope where C fails." Spend the full budget. Log the transcript; an unlogged attempt is no attempt.
4. **Adjudicate.** A kill counts only as a reproducible demonstration of pre-registered K, re-executed by a party paid the same either way. Reproduces → BROKEN, lineage recorded; the revised claim keeps its ancestor's graveyard entry and re-enters at UNTESTED. Fails to reproduce → the failed reproduction is logged and the attack continues.
5. **Count.** T(C) = distinct failure modes competently attacked and survived, per the countersigned enumeration; variants of one mechanism count once, and the mode-distinctness review (Evaluation 7) reverts inflated counts. Heads, rounds, and confirmations add zero.

**THRESHOLD**
Tier 1: T ≥ 1. Tier 2: T ≥ 3 as specified above. These numbers are stipulated policy parameters, not discovered truths — calibrated by tightening until stings are caught at the target rate, and owed no reverence beyond their sting record.

**OUTPUT**
Exactly one of **UNTESTABLE / UNTESTED / SURVIVED-n (scope, date) / BROKEN (artifact)**. The label travels with the claim, binds to the scope attacked, and decays on pre-declared material change classes. The full record — K, refuter payoffs, budget, transcripts, plant results — is published so anyone can re-attack without permission. Trust is the record. Nothing else is.

**Worked check:** a model reporting its own token counts and grading its own quality — every item in the bundle is self-report, deleted at step 2, T = 0, UNTESTED at any tier above 0. A year of "6/6 validations," decided in one pass.

**DRAFT-K, second version.** The first version was killed by the gate panel, and the kill is recorded per clause 4's own lineage requirement: at 20–50 claims per arm, its 25%-reduction-at-90%-confidence bound convicted a genuinely working harness most of the time — a refuter that kills true plants, void by Procedure step 1 — and it measured "downstream falsification" under unequal surveillance, since SURVIVED claims publish records inviting re-attack while control claims sit unwatched. The replacement:

- **Burden direction, stated.** BROKEN is a harm verdict, not an absence-of-proof verdict. A per-adopter record posts BROKEN only when the data show, at 90% one-sided confidence, that claims this harness labeled SURVIVED falsify at a rate equal to or higher than that adopter's matched control. A record showing neither harm nor benefit is UNRESOLVED: it stays open, pools forward, and the probation cap stays on. Insufficient data never converts to BROKEN.
- **The benefit claim must buy its power.** The rule earns SURVIVED only when the record pooled across adopters shows a ≥25% relative reduction in falsification rate at 90% one-sided confidence, with the event floor set for 80% power against that effect at the pooled base rate — on the order of hundreds of claims per arm at realistic falsification rates, not twenty. Until then the rule's label is exactly UNTESTED, whatever any single record says.
- **Window, set at countersignature.** Every adoption bet carries a window — calendar or gated-claim count — set by the watcher at countersignature, never by the bettor. At window close the record resolves or posts UNRESOLVED; UNRESOLVED rolls forward only on fresh watcher countersignature, and a record that rolls twice unresolved freezes the adopter's probation cap until it resolves. A bet with no window is no bet.
- **One instrument, both arms.** Falsification is not measured in the wild. At each resolution point the watcher assembles a blinded, shuffled sample from both arms, matched on tier and budget, and commissions equal-budget re-attacks through the steward topology; the falsification rate is the break rate under this common instrument, and neither arm is identifiable to the attackers. For this measurement alone, Evaluation 5's label mandate yields to the blind: the watcher strips labels, records, and lineage from both arms before hand-off and logs the strip in the bet record — the label mandate governs claims in use, and a blinded re-attack sample is not a use. Where prior publication makes a perfect blind impossible, the watcher logs the exposure and the analysis plan states the record's reduced weight in advance.
- **Control construction.** Adopters with a prior vetting process: pre-adoption claims from their own ledger, matched on tier and cost — already relied on before the rule governed them, so the comparison creates no new violations. Adopters with no prior record are **monitoring-only**: their records feed neither the harm verdict nor the benefit verdict, at any level, and never count among the death clause's three. Monitoring-only status does not expire on its own — the one conversion path is pre-registered randomization: during probation the watcher assigns eligible sub-Tier-2 claims at random between the attack lane and the bet lane, both fully governed, and the bet-lane arm serves as comparator under the common blinded instrument. No randomization, no conversion — and no ungoverned comparison arm exists under any name.
- **Death clause.** The rule is BROKEN when the pooled matched-control record shows harm at the stated confidence, or when three independent matched-control adopter records each post the harm verdict. Monitoring-only and UNRESOLVED records count toward nothing, in either direction. The mirror, so the signal is never quiet by construction: until at least three matched-control adopter records exist, the rule cannot claim pooled SURVIVED at any effect size — its label stays UNTESTED however many monitoring-only adopters accumulate.

These numbers remain stipulated policy parameters — adjusted only in the open, by the watcher side, never retroactively.

Until adoption exists, the label is exact: **UNTESTED — crossed at Tier 0 under a pre-authored standing table, full attack record attached, standing invitation open.** Not SURVIVED. Not exempt.

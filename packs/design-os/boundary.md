# Boundary — Design OS

**Boundary · permanent**

What stays human in this pack. The xOPs in this pack govern conduct — brief fidelity, layout
judgment, emphasis, critique quality, evidence. They do not and cannot govern taste.

---

## What stays human

**Aesthetic quality.** Is it beautiful? Does it feel premium? Is the brand personality right?
Does it move me? These are not xOP questions. They cannot be anchored to an observable that
an independent reviewer could label without sharing the same taste.

**Brand direction.** What is the brand? What does it stand for? What emotion should it produce?
These are human decisions, made by a person with authority. An xOP can preserve a brand decision
once made (via `xop.decision.ledger`, profile: positioning). It cannot make the decision.

**Creative judgment.** Is this the right concept? Is this the right metaphor? Is this the right
move for this moment? These require the kind of judgment that comes from knowing the work,
the audience, and the context at a depth a rule cannot reach.

**Taste as evidence.** "I don't like it" is not a defect. `xop.design.critique-evidence` stops
the AI from presenting taste as objective error — but it does not stop you from having taste.
Your taste is a legitimate input; it is not a checkable basis for a design critique.

---

## The conduct / taste boundary (plain language)

> Design systems tell AI what the brand is made of.
> Skills teach it how to do the work.
> xOPs govern the moments where judgment matters — when to preserve, adapt, challenge, ask, stop.
> *Creative xOPs do not automate taste; they stop AI from overriding the brief, the evidence,
> the system, or the human decision without a reason.*

When in doubt: if the question is "is this good?" it's a Boundary. If the question is "did this
preserve the brief / use warranted evidence / serve the slide's job?" it's an xOP.

---

*This boundary is permanent. No xOP in this pack overrides it.*
